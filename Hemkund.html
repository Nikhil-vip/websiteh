About Hemkund Sahib

Hemkund Sahib is a revered Sikh pilgrimage site located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. It is situated at an altitude of 4,632 meters (15,197 feet) and is surrounded by seven snow-capped peaks. The site is known for its serene beauty and the sacred Hemkund Lake, which is believed to have healing properties.

The Gurudwara at Hemkund Sahib was established in 1936 by Sant Sewa Singh, who had a vision of Guru Gobind Singh Ji, the tenth Sikh Guru. The Gurudwara attracts thousands of pilgrims every year, especially during the summer months when the weather is more favorable for trekking.
